Because \(\theta\) and \(180\degree + \theta\) have the same reference angle, they have the same tangent. For example, \begin{align*} \tan 200\degree \amp = \tan 20\degree\\ \tan 230\degree \amp = \tan 50\degree\\ \tan 250\degree \amp = \tan 70\degree \end{align*} Thus, the graph of \(\tan \theta\) in the third quadrant is the same as its graph in the first quadrant.
