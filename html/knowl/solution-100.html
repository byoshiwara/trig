SolutionA.114.1

  1. Let \(x\) represent the length of the shortest side, so that the third side has length \(2x\text{.}\) (See FigureÂ A.115.)

  2. Substituting these expressions into the Pythagorean theorem, we find

    \begin{equation*} x^2+(2x)^2=15^2 \end{equation*}

  3. This is a quadratic equation with no linear term, so we simplify and then isolate \(x^2\text{.}\)

    \begin{align*} x^2+4x^2\amp =225\amp\amp\text{Combine like terms.} \\ 5x^2\amp = 225\amp\amp\text{Divide both sides by 5.} \\ x^2\amp = 45 \end{align*}

    Taking square roots of both sides yields

    \begin{equation*} x=\pm\sqrt{45}\approx\pm 6.708203932 \end{equation*}

  4. Because a length must be a positive number, the shortest side has length approximately \(6.71\) feet, and the third side has length \(2 (6.71)\text{,}\) or approximately \(13.42\) feet.
