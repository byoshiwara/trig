  1. We subtract multiples of \(360\degree\) from \(520\degree\) until the remainder is less than \(360\degree\text{.}\)
    \begin{equation*} 520\degree - 360\degree = 160\degree \end{equation*}
    Because \(160\degree\) is between \(0\degree\) and \(360\degree\text{,}\) this is the angle we want.
  2. Enter SIN 160 and SIN 520 to see that
    \begin{equation*} \sin 160\degree = \sin 520\degree = 0.3420 \end{equation*}
    Enter COS 160 and COS 520 to see that
    \begin{equation*} \cos 160\degree = \cos 520\degree = -0.9397 \end{equation*}
    Enter TAN 160 and TAN 520 to see that
    \begin{equation*} \tan 160\degree = \tan 520\degree = -0.3640 \end{equation*}
