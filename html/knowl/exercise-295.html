Exercise43

In this problem, we'll show that any angle inscribed in a semi-circle must be a right angle. The figure shows a triangle inscribed in a unit circle, one side lying on the diameter of the circle and the opposite vertex at point \((p,q)\) on the circle.

  1. What are the coordinates of the other two vertices of the triangle? What is the length of the side joining those vertices?
  2. Use the distance formula to compute the lengths of the other two sides of the triangle.
  3. Show that the sides of the triangle satisfy the Pythagorean theorem, \(a^2 + b^2 = c^2\text{.}\)
