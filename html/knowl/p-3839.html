  1. Keep in mind that \(\arccos \dfrac{1}{3}\) is an angle. We'll call it \(\theta\text{,}\) so that \(\theta = \arccos \dfrac{1}{3}\text{,}\) and hence \(\cos \theta = \dfrac{1}{3}\text{.}\) Then substituting \(\theta\) for \(\arccos \dfrac{1}{3}\) in the original expression gives us
    \begin{equation*} \cos(\arccos \dfrac{1}{3}) = \cos \theta = \dfrac{1}{3} \end{equation*}

We start inside the parentheses: \(\sin \dfrac{3\pi}{2} = -1\text{.}\) So \(\arcsin(\sin \dfrac{3\pi}{2}) = \arcsin(-1)\text{.}\) Now \(\arcsin(-1)\) is the angle between \(\dfrac{-\pi}{2}\) and 0 whose sine is \(-1\text{,}\) and that angle is \(\dfrac{-\pi}{2}\text{.}\) Thus, \(\arcsin(\sin \dfrac{3\pi}{2}) = \dfrac{-\pi}{2}\)
