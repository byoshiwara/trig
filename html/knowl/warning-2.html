Caution1.15

Note that when you press the sine key, SIN , your calculator displays

\begin{equation*} \text{sin}~ ( \end{equation*}

with an open parenthesis, as the prompt to enter an angle. It is more proper to use parentheses and write sin \((50\degree)\) for “the sine of \(50\degree\text{,}\)” but we often omit the parentheses in simple trigonometric expressions.

Think of the notation sin as an operation symbol telling you to find the sine of an angle, just as the symbol \(\sqrt{~~} \) tells you to take the square root of the expression under the radical.
