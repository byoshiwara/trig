Activity 6.2. Trigonometry in Radians.

Here is a unit circle with arclengths labeled, measured counterclockwise from \((1,0)\text{.}\) (Note that the distance around the whole circle is 6.28 units!) In Section 6.1 we learned that on a unit circle, the measure of a positive angle in radians is equal to the length of the arc that it spans.
  1. Use the unit circle to estimate the sine, cosine, and tangent of each angle in radians.
    1. \(\displaystyle 0.6\)
    2. \(\displaystyle 2.3\)
    3. \(\displaystyle 3.5\)
    4. \(\displaystyle 5.3\)
  2. Use the unit circle to estimate two solutions to each equation.
    1. \(\displaystyle \cos (\theta) = 0.3\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \sin (\theta) = 0.7\)
  3. Sketch the angle on the unit circle. Use symmetry to find three more angles with the same sine and cosine, up to sign.
    1. \(\displaystyle 1.8\)
    2. \(\displaystyle 5.2\)
    3. \(\displaystyle 3.7\)
    4. \(\displaystyle 0.3\)
