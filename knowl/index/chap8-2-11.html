The horizontal scale factor at any latitude must be inversely proportional to lengths on that latitude. Because the radius of the circle of latitude \(\theta\) is \(R \cos \theta\text{,}\) the corresponding parallel on the map must be stretched by a factor of \(\dfrac{1}{\cos \theta}\text{.}\) And because the secant is the reciprocal of the cosine, the scale factor is proportional to the secant of the latitude.
