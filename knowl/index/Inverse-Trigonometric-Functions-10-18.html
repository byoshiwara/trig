Inverse Trigonometric Functions.

\begin{align*} \sin\left(\sin^{-1}(x)\right) \amp = x~~~~\text{for}~ -1 \le x \le 1\\ \cos\left(\cos^{-1}(x)\right) \amp = x~~~~\text{for}~ -1 \le x \le 1\\ \tan\left(\tan^{-1}(x)\right) \amp = x~~~~\text{for all}~x \end{align*}
However,
\begin{equation*} \sin^{-1}\left(\sin (x)\right)~~\text{may not be equal to}~x \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \cos^{-1}\left(\cos (x)\right)~~\text{may not be equal to}~x \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \tan^{-1}\left(\tan (x)\right)~~\text{may not be equal to}~x \end{equation*}
