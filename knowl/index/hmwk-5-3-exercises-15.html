Exercise 78.

Prove the tangent identity \(\tan (\theta) = \dfrac{\sin (\theta)}{\cos (\theta)}\) by carrying out the following steps. Sketch an angle \(\theta\) in standard position, and label a point \((x,y)\) on the terminal side, at a distance \(r\) from the vertex.
  1. Write \(\sin (\theta)\) in terms of \(y\) and \(r\text{,}\) and solve for \(y\text{.}\)
  2. Write \(\cos (\theta)\) in terms of \(x\) and \(r\text{,}\) and solve for \(x\text{.}\)
  3. Write \(\tan (\theta)\) in terms of \(x\) and \(y\text{,}\) then substitute your results from parts (a) and (b).
  4. Simplify your fraction in part (c).
