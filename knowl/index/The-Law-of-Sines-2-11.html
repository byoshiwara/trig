Law of Sines.

If the angles of a triangle are \(A, B\text{,}\) and \(C\text{,}\) and the opposite sides are respectively \(a, b,\) and \(c\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} \blert{\dfrac {\sin (A)}{a} = \dfrac {\sin (B)}{b} = \dfrac {\sin (C)}{c}} \end{equation*}
or equivalently,
\begin{equation*} \blert{\dfrac {a}{\sin (A)} = \dfrac {b}{\sin (B)} = \dfrac {c}{\sin (C)}} \end{equation*}
